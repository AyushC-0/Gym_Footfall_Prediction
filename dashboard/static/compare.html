warning WHAT HAPPENED IN 2026: DATA DRIFT

On January 1, 2026, WeGoGym changed its operating hours. Instead of running 3-hour sessions (6, 7, 8 AM & 5, 6, 7 PM), management compressed hours to two 2-hour slots: 7-9 AM and 6-8 PM. This operational shift immediately caused our highly accurate V2 models to catastrophically fail on live data. Here is why:

1. Covariate Shift (Feature Drift)

The underlying distribution of the input data fundamentally changed. The 6 AM and 5 PM (17:00) hours were completely removed from the incoming dataset. Our model was trained to weigh these variables heavily, but their probability dropped to exactly 0%.

2. Concept Drift (Behavioral Shift)

Because available gym hours became 33% shorter, the same gym-going student population was squeezed into heavily constrained time windows. The relationship between active student population and peak-hour density completely broke. A crowd of 50 students that used to spread evenly over 3 hours now packs tightly into 2 hours, generating massive surges that the model never learned to expect.

model_training

How We Fixed It (V3 Robust Pipeline)

Simply throwing new data at the model isn't enough to fix architectural failures. To generate our highly resilient V3 datasets and models (*_v3.pkl), we fundamentally redesigned the machine learning pipeline:

1. Context Features

We injected a total_daily_slots parameter directly into the preprocessor. Instead of letting the tree models arbitrarily guess why density spiked, they now mathematically understand that 4 available slots intrinsically means higher crowding than 6.

2. Sample Weighting

We applied dynamic sample weights during model.fit(). The algorithm was forced to prioritize learning the new 2026 post-drift behaviors (3x weight) while effectively "forgetting" the obsolete 2021-2025 student scheduling habits.

3. Strict Temporal Splits

We removed random train-test splitting (which leaks future data). V3 was trained exclusively on past data (2021 to mid-2026) and validated strictly against unseen future data (late 2026 to 2027) to guarantee real-world generalization.
